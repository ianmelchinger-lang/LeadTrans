Score one AI use case

Every AI leader ends up with more candidate projects than budget. A list ranked by gut feeling is just opinion with a sort order. A weighted composite score — one number built from several factors, each counted by how much it should matter — is what lets a leader defend the ranking to a CFO or risk committee, factor by factor.

Work through this at your own pace. There's nothing to submit and nothing tracked — close the tab whenever you're done.

A few terms first

  • Business value — how much this AI idea could realistically move revenue, cost, or a decision that matters.
  • Feasibility — how ready the organization is to actually build and run it: the data, the people, the timeline.
  • Risk — what could go wrong: a bad decision, legal exposure, reputational damage. Below it's scored inverse, so a high risk score means low risk.
  • Weight — how much a factor counts toward the final score, shown as a share of the whole.
  • Composite score — one overall number: each factor's score multiplied by its weight, then all six added together.
  • Band — what the composite score means for action: fund it now, look closer, or set it aside.
