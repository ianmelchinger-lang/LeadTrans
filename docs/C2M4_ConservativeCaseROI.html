Does the conservative case clear breakeven?

A CFO reads the conservative scenario first and treats everything above it as upside. Run the numbers for one process and find out whether this case survives its own toughest test.

Before the formula, four words worth pinning down. A baseline is the measured, current-state cost, time, and volume of a process — pulled from real records, never guessed. Total cost of ownership (TCO) is everything it costs to run the AI solution: not just the software price, but data preparation, integration, training, and a contingency buffer. A realization rate is the share of the theoretical benefit that actually shows up in year one, because adoption never hits 100% on day one. And the conservative case is the scenario built at the lowest realistic realization rate, with full costs included — it's the number the CFO trusts, because everything above it is upside, not the floor.

The formula (from the module's own worked method):

ROI = (Cost Savings − Total Costs) ÷ Total Costs × 100

The numbers below are a sample process, built to practice the method — on the job, you'd swap in your own measured baseline and your own finance team's cost figures.
