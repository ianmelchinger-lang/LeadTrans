Where does this belong: Phase One or Phase Two?

Practice the core move behind sequencing an AI roadmap: deciding what an organization can absorb right now, not just what is technically possible.

The situation, in plain terms

Imagine a leader has a short list of AI initiatives ready to try — say, a support chatbot, a forecasting model, and a document-processing tool. The tempting question is "which one can we build fastest?" The better question is the absorption question: can the organization actually absorb this initiative right now — not just build it, but run it, govern it, and get people to use it?

Every candidate gets checked against four conditions. A data pipeline is the flow of information the initiative needs already connected and usable. Governance means someone is authorized to approve, monitor, and take responsibility for it. Team capacity means the people who'd run or adopt it have the time and training to do so. An integration dependency is a connection to an existing system or workflow that has to be built or confirmed first.

Each condition gets a quick read: green (ready), yellow (a gap, but a manageable one), or red (not ready — a blocking dependency). A candidate that clears this bar goes in Phase One, the work started now. One that doesn't goes in Phase Two, held back until the missing foundation is built. When a placement is unclear, the momentum test settles it: would succeeding here build enough confidence and evidence to fund what comes next, or does it still need Phase-Two groundwork first?

The rule this exercise uses:
  • Any red condition = a blocking dependency. The initiative goes to Phase Two, no exceptions — technical feasibility doesn't override an unresolved blocker.
  • Two or more yellow conditions, with no red = the foundations aren't solid enough yet, even though nothing is fully blocked. Phase Two.
  • One yellow or none, with no red = the organization can absorb it now. Phase One — any single yellow travels with it as a named mitigation, not a blocker.
