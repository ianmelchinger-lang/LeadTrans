Priority or experiment?

A five-minute sort using the test that keeps a strategy from turning into a pile of pilots.

When a company decides to "use AI," ideas show up from everywhere: a vendor's pitch, an executive's favorite demo, a team's own good instinct. Not all of them deserve funding. A real AI priority is an idea that ties back to a business objective the company is already committed to, and to a specific number — a KPI, short for key performance indicator — that tells you whether it's working. An experiment is everything else: a good idea that hasn't been tied to a goal or a number yet.
